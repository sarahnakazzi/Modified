Lion:
The lion is a large cat of the genus Panthera, native to Africa and India. It has a muscular, broad-chested body; a short, rounded head; round ears; and a hairy tuff at the end of its tail. It is sexually dimorphic; adult male lions are larger than females and have a prominentmane. It is a social species, forming groups called prides.
A lion's pride consists of a few adults males, related females and cubs.

Leopard:
The leopard one of the five extant species in the genuis Panthera. It has a pale yellowish to dark golden fur with dark spots grouped in rosettes. Its body is slender and muscular.<

Antelope:
An antelope is a shift-running deerlike ruminant with smooth hair and upward-pointing horns, of a group native to Africa and Asia that includes the gazelles, impala, gnus, and elands.
